Work Practices/Team Norms

Team Norms [DRAFT]

These suggested norms are based on a collaborative workshop between DSVA and Ad Hoc on Oct 17, 2018

  1. Constantly and openly communicate - be willing to hop on the phone if slack/email aren't being effective
  2. Engage in non-judgemental communication
  3. Communicate transparently early and often - sharing what we do and don't know is better than waiting for all the answers before sharing any info.
  4. Make sure all members of the team feel heard
  5. Use shared struggle to build communication and trust, not to foster fear and blame
  6. Don't be random - have clear sense of direction
  7. Be driven by single purpose
  8. Don't compete with one another
  9. Treat people as humans, not interchangeable assets
  10. Don't be immature / lack commitment
  11. Leave ego at the door
  12. Recognize and appreciate peoples' work, instead of trying to squeeze more out of them
  13. Remove the stigma of failure by creating a respectful blameless culture that encourages taking risk and getting small things out early
  14. Assume best intentions - especially important on remote teams
  15. Trust one another and ask questions freely
  16. Form a collective BS umbrella - one person shieding the team, but also shield/support each other
